Rules of Project Room (G02)

We are implementing new features in the room G02 on the G/F, starting with a 52" TV. The purpose of this room will be both academic and for entertainment,and hence, G02 shall be named "Project Room". You will be able to use it for meetings, to rehearse for your presentations or other activities, such as screening movies with your friends or floor mates.

To maintain a good and fair use of this room, we have developed the following rules:

1. To have access the project room, a resident of Hall 9 will have to make a booking by writing their name, student ID number, date, time and duration at the security counter.
2. The maximum number of bookings allowed for each resident during any given week is 2 (two) bookings a week, for a maximum of 3 (three) hours each time.
3. The booking availability of the room shall be from 9:00 am to 12:00 am (midnight).
4. During the revision periods as announced by the SRO, the availability of the project room will be extended till 2 AM. All the other rules and regulations will stay unaffected.
5. The users of Project Room should respect the quiet hours of the Hall (that is from 11:00 PM to 07:00 AM).
6. The security guard will hand the key to the resident, so he/she can unlock the door to the project room. The key is to be returned to the security counter after the booking period expires.
7. The resident who makes the booking is fully responsible of the handling of the equipment, of keeping the room organized and tidy and locking the door after finishing their activity.
8. NO device shall be connected directly to the TV. A USB cord will be provided [to the so that the device can be connected to the TV through it.
9. The project room shall remain locked while it is not in use.

Important: The resident booking the project room should check the functionality of the equipment in the room. Any malfunction shall be reported within 10 (ten) minutes from the start of the booking period to the security guard. Any subsequent malfunctions should be reported immediately to the security guard.



Rules of Computer Room (LG02)

The Equipment in the Computer Room is provided by SRO. The use of the facilities at the Computer Rooms of the Student Residence is restricted to all students and staff of the City University of Hong Kong. Priority of use is given to the residents of the respective hall. 2. All users, upon request, must produce appropriate ID cards for inspection by the security guard or the SRO staff on duty.

You have to follow the "Regulations for use of the Computer rooms":

1. Opening Hours: 8:00am - 2:00am (Mon - Sun); 2. Don't eat, drink, or play any form of game;
3. Don't cause any disturbance to others (e.g. shouting, singing, etc.);
4. Don't install, alter, delete, or copy any software on computers provided by SRO;
5. Don't allow others to use his/her account(s);
6. Don't use any equipment for non-academic related work;
7. Don't connect any device to the equipment in the Computer Rooms without prior approval, or
8. Don't remove, deface or damage any equipment, manual, or other property of the SRO.



Rules of Pianno Room (G03)

Residents of the Student Residence may make use of the Piano Rooms for practice. User should also comply with the Regulations Governing Student Residence and the Hall Rules formulated by each hall.

You have to follow the "Regulations for Use of the Piano Rooms":

1. Opening Hours: 9:00am - 22:00pm (Mon - Sun);
2. User should approach the Security Counter with his/ her student I.D. card and resident card and report to the Counter after using the Piano Rooms;
3. User should not transfer the booking/ usage to other university members or visitors;
4. User should NOT bring along any visitors without prior permission;
5. User should NOT make use of the Piano Rooms for private tuition of musical instruments;
6. User should not move any musical instruments in Piano Rooms without prior permission;
7. User should not leave the room unattended at any time during the booked period;
8. Any damage of musical instruments, furniture or fixtures and fittings in the rooms should be reported to the SRO at once. In case of misuse, negligence or vandalism, user will be held responsible for the total cost incurred for repair or replacement.
