Visit to Kam Shan Country Park

On November 7, 2021, the wuddies broke out of the bustling city and set out to conquer the hilly peaks of Hong Kong. Having gathered at exactly 10 am, our company of 16 people went on a hike in the Kam Shan country park. First we took the mtr to Prince Edward station and then got on the bus. After half an hour we were already standing at the dam. The weather was sunny, it was even hot. The sky was blue and almost cloudless. At the beginning of the trip, we familiarized ourselves with the rules and tried not to provoke the little thieves with food or our valuables. The route we chose was the easiest - Family Walk. Together with the group, we mastered it in 2 hours or less. In addition, in order to admire the local landscapes at the beginning of the hike, the organizers announced a photography competition, so each participant tried to get bright and dynamic photos in order to get the opportunity to win ParknShop coupons. At the end of the hike, everyone was tired, but happy. Following the same route, everyone returned safely to the student residence. This SIA was remembered for the beauty of Hong Kong's nature and allowed students who became part of the Hall 9 recently to get to know each other and get to know the city a little better.

DIY Boba Tea Workshop

Boba tea is a Taiwanese snack, in which people add chewy tapioca balls (also known as,'boba') to milk tea. In Taiwan, this appetizer can be bought almost everywhere, ranging from small vendors in the countryside to famous restaurants. It is so important in Taiwanese people's life that this drink becomes an important culture in Taiwan. Today, two Taiwanese residents, Cindy and Daisy, showcased this traditional snack to the Wuddies. The SIA's main objective was to teach participants how to make boba tea. First of all, we made our own boba and added it to the milk tea. To do it, we boiled water with some brown sugar, then added this boiled mixture immediately into tapioca powder. Next, mixed them, made a piece of dough out of it, and tried to give these pieces a round, pearl-like shape - 'boba-shape'. Then we boiled the 'bobas' for 15 minutes and put them in exactly when the water was boiled. Otherwise, the boba would dissolve in cool water. After that, we turned off the stove and simmered the boba for 15 minutes. Finally, the boba is ready to enjoy! Handmade 'boba' was not only chewy and delicious, most importantly, it was made of wholesome ingredients, and it was healthier than the 'boba' sold in convenient shops. Everyone enjoyed their boba tea!

Jazz Dance Workshop

Hall 9's Next Cooking Mama/ Papa?

The first SIA of hall 9 'Cooking Mama/Papa' was held by the Indonesian residents. During the event, residents learned how to cook Indonesian cuisine, a stuffed fried tofu. Four groups of five cooking enthusiasts were formed and distributed over four floors. Now we will briefly introduce the recipe. First, we stir-fried some vegetables for the stuffing, such as carrots, bean sprouts, cabbage, garlic and onions. Some spices, such as turmeric and pepper, were also added into the stuffing to enhance the flavor. After that, we removed the center of tofu puffs, and stuffed the vegetables into the empty puffs. Next, we made batter using eggs, flour, water and spices; also, prepared bread crumps for breading. After dipping the delicious tofu into batter, we covered them in bread crumps and deep-fried them till turning gold and appetizing. During the event, the residents were able to become acquainted and have a pleasant conversation. Also, our new Residence Master, Denvid, participated briefly and took some photos of the residents. He introduced himself in high spirits and encouraged us to ask for help if there is any problem. Right after preparing the dish, all the participants gathered at the 8th floor common room for a little party. The results of cooking were fantastic, the savory taste of the stuffing combined with the crispy crust of tofu puffs worked just great. Everyone had great fun and were happy to taste the authentic Indonesian snack.

Jack-o-Lantern

Pumpkin carving is a long-standing Halloween tradition enjoyed by millions of people world-wide every year. The fun begins by cutting off the top of the pumpkin, from which the slimy insides are briskly scooped out. Once you have a hollowed-out form, you may begin carefully carving whatever monstrous or comical face you get inspired by. On the 28th of October, 2017, the residents of Hall 9 were lucky enough to experience this pleasure!

Though it was hard work, everyone was well rewarded by the quirky and creative results. Many took pictures to capture the iconic event, freezing it in time forever. By the end of the night, the pumpkins got to return with their owners, casting a spooky Halloween aura in their new homes.

Customize your Indoensian Pancake

Initiated by Hall 9 Indonesian residents, the activity "Customize your Indonesian Pancake" introduced their traditional pancake "Martabak", a popular dish in the streets of Indonesia.

First, we mixed all the ingredients: flour, activated dried yeast, baking powder, baking soda, eggs, milk, melted margarine and water. To start baking, we poured butter and the dough evenly to the pan, added sugar and butter on the dough surface, closed the pan and waited until the dough puffed up. Then, peanut butter, chocolate sprinkles and condensed milk were put on the pancake according to our preference. The soft and hot pancake within smelled tempting!

Flowers on Tongue

Flower cake is a traditional Yunnan flaky pastry with some edible roses in the centre. It is not only the representative dessert of China, but also one of the representative of Yunnan-style mooncakes.

Students got the chance to try to make their own flower cakes from scratch. They rolled the dough, put in the edible rose as the filling and shaped the dough to fully wrap the rose. The dough was baked for 20 minutes. After a while, an enticing smell spread through the whole room and the whole process was finished then! Everyone was satisfied with their own work and enjoyed a lot in this activity.

Korean Tteokbokki Night

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean snack, well loved by teenagers and adults. It is made using garaetteok - a chewy, long cylindrical rice pasta; it's cut into many pieces and stir-fried with various ingredients such as the gochujang (red chili pepper paste), fish cakes and assorted vegetables (onions, etc).

Wuddies gathered ahead of time and discussed Tteokbokki excitedly. The smell of onions has permeated the common room of the fifth floor even before the activity started.

Let's learn Korean

After the long Chinese New Year Holiday, Korean Wuddies initiated "Korean Learning Day" for Hall 9 non-Korean speakers. There were 6 tutors, so we divided the residence into 6 groups.

Korean "Hangeul" was made with the combinations of Korean alphabets. Basically, Korean have 14 consonants and 10 vowels. By combining of those consonants and vowels, Korean can be written and read. The participants learnt the basic Korean consonants, vowels and some valuable expressions for travelling Korea or conversation with Koreans.

It was really valuable time for residence for experiencing the different culture through their languages.
